The Dilemma

An Interactive Guide to the Orientation Game

1. Executive Summary

The GEC Dilemma is an experiential learning game designed to provide freshers with a powerful lesson on the critical importance of collaboration, trust, and long-term thinking. Through a simple, engaging simulation, it highlights how individual decisions, while seemingly beneficial in the short term, can negatively impact the collective success of the entire organization.

2. Game Concept & Core Mechanics

The game's theme places participants as four competing internal teams (A, B, C, D) within a large company, GEC Inc. The ultimate objective is for all teams to contribute to the overall success of the company, not just their own scores.

The central conflict, or The Dilemma, is presented in each round: teams must choose between cooperating (the Red Card) or acting selfishly (the Black Card). The total company score is a direct result of the collective choices made by all four teams, demonstrating in real-time that a lack of trust and cooperation inevitably leads to a negative outcome for everyone involved.

4. Game Flow & Logistics

  1. 1
    Internal Discussion (30s):

    Teams convene privately to strategize their card choice for the round.

  2. 2
    Representative Meeting (60s):

    A representative from each team meets in a central location to negotiate a collective, collaborative strategy. This is where trust is built or broken.

  3. 3
    Final Decision (30s):

    Representatives return to their teams for the final, private decision. The tension lies in whether teams will honor the negotiated agreement.

  4. 4
    Reveal & Scoring:

    Teams reveal their cards simultaneously. The scores are calculated based on the collective result and updated on the scoreboard.

5. Debriefing & Key Takeaways

The debrief is the most vital part of the event. It is during this time that the game's message is reinforced. Begin by asking open-ended questions about how participants felt, their thought processes, and their decision-making. Reveal the stark contrast between the final company score and the potential score if everyone had consistently cooperated.

Explain that the game is a metaphor for real-world projects and business. Emphasize that in a truly successful organization, prioritizing an individual's "win" over the collective good is a form of sabotage that leads to shared failure. True success is built on collaboration and trust, and not on selfish, short-term gains.

To reinforce this, the prize consists of refreshments for all participants. There is no single winner; the prize is awarded to everyone for "taking the lesson." This highlights that the greatest reward is the shared experience and valuable knowledge gained together.
